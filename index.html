Why is the dataset collected?

This dataset is collected to outline the diversities by country of birth in Australia.

 

Which government department do you think will use this?

Such type of dataset would be useful for the Department of Immigration and Border Protection.

 

Which private sector will use this?

This dataset could be used within private corporations.

 

How would this dataset change the way you think about space?

It allows governmental bodies to analyse the use of space based on cultural differences. For instance, it could be an indicator of where cultural facilities should be placed.

 

How does this dataset change across Sydney?

Those of ethnic origin (Non-western Europeans) tend to stay closer to each other in select clusters around Sydney. Whilst those of Western European decent are more likely to spread out with no identifiable clusters, with the majority closer to the city.

 

How does it relate to the latte line?

Most residents from regions other than Europe tend to live south of the latte line. This can be stemmed from the fact that many of these residents are first wave immigrants with naturally lower pay, resulting in the lack of on-the-go coffees.

 

How has the dataset changed between 2011 and 2016?

The only significant changes are the slight increase of cluster density, with more people of the same ethnicity residing in the same area; and the expansion of most clusters.

 

How is this dataset significate to people’s lives?

It allows both long-time citizens or those fresh off the boat know where their ethnic group is most likely located, allowing the feeling of a 'home away from home' with those of the same ethnicity.

 

Would it be good for this data to change?

Not necessarily, the dataset effectively shows location of ethnic populations through years of arrival.

 

If you wanted to change it, how would you change it?

If there was to be a change, it would be most likely reducing the years covered in one dataset. It will provide a far more detailed insight into when immigrants came into Australia.
